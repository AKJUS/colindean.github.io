About

Colin Dean is a software engineer, journalist, and non-profit leader based in Pittsburgh. By day, he engineers software as a part of the IBM Watson Group, specifically on the Watson Explorer product team. He previously delivered professional services and consultation on Watson Explorer, formerly known as InfoSphere Data Explorer and Vivísimo Velocity.

Otherwise, he writes unbiased computer and consumer electronics hardware and software reviews for ThinkComputers and organizes computer and console video gaming events as part of Pittsburgh LAN Coalition, a.k.a Pittco. He contributes to a wide variety of open source projects and community activites, such as Steel City Ruby Conference and DevOpsDays Pittsburgh. He is also involved in Meta Mesh and its creation, Pittmesh, a wireless mesh network that will eventually span the greater Pittsburgh area.

Aside from video gaming and software, his interests include linguistics with a focus on Esperanto, libertarian political philosophy, photography, Bitcoin, and a growing appreciation for dogs.
